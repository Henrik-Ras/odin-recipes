Description

Beef Stroganoff is a Russian dish consisting of tender beef strips cooked with onions and mushrooms in a creamy sour cream sauce, offering a comforting and flavorful meal typically served over egg noodles or rice. It's celebrated for its rich texture and harmonious blend of savory flavors.

Ingredients

  • Beef (such as sirloin or tenderloin), thinly sliced
  • Onions, thinly sliced
  • Mushrooms, sliced
  • Sour cream
  • Beef broth or stock
  • Butter or oil
  • Garlic, minced (optional)
  • Flour (for thickening the sauce)
  • Worcestershire sauce (optional)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Egg noodles or rice (for serving)

  1. Thinly slice the beef, onions, and mushrooms. Mince the garlic if using.
  2. In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of butter or oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef slices and cook until browned on all sides, then transfer them to a plate.
  3. In the same skillet, add the remaining tablespoon of butter or oil. Add the sliced onions and mushrooms, and cook until they are softened and lightly browned, about 5-7 minutes. If using garlic, add it during the last minute of cooking.
  4. Sprinkle the flour over the onions and mushrooms, stirring to coat evenly. Cook for 1-2 minutes to remove the raw flour taste.
  5. Gradually pour in the beef broth, stirring constantly to prevent lumps from forming. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook until thickened, about 5 minutes.
  6. Once the sauce has thickened, return the browned beef slices to the skillet. Stir in the Worcestershire sauce if using. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Reduce the heat to low and let the mixture simmer gently for another 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  8. Turn off the heat and stir in the sour cream until it's well incorporated and the sauce is creamy.
  9. Serve the Beef Stroganoff hot over cooked egg noodles or rice. Garnish with parsley if desired.
